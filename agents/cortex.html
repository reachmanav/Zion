🔮 ChromaDB — Vector Search (Semantic)
What it does
ChromaDB stores 349,000 text chunks from 20GB of documents — but not as plain text. Each chunk is converted into a 384-dimension number vector (an "embedding").

These vectors capture meaning, not words. So when you search, it finds chunks that are about the same thing — even if they use completely different words.
The question: "@neo what do you mean by F1 DRS?"
ChromaDB searches 349K chunks for meaning. No doc says "F1 DRS" directly — but these are the closest matches:

📄 "The automobile engine burns fuel at 15,000 RPM..." 0.71
📄 "Aerodynamic wind tunnel testing for racing..." 0.65
📄 "Vehicle maintenance procedures..." 0.58

These are related to racing but none actually explain DRS. GPT-4.1 stitches them together and gets:

😐 Answer #1: "DRS is related to aerodynamic systems and rear wings..."

Garbled. Didn't answer the question.
⚡ Trinity enriches → uploads back to ChromaDB
🔮 Same question AFTER Enrich — new scores
Trinity wrote a deep explanation and loaded it into ChromaDB. Now:

💎 "DRS = Drag Reduction System, rear wing flap, +12 km/h, eliminated 2026..." 0.94
📄 "The automobile engine burns fuel at 15,000 RPM..." 0.71
📄 "Aerodynamic wind tunnel testing..." 0.65

The enriched doc is now #1 at 0.94. GPT gets the perfect source and gives:

🔍 SQLite FTS5 — Keyword Search
What it does
FTS5 is Full-Text Search — the old-school way. It indexes every word in every document and finds exact matches.

Fast, precise, and catches things that semantic search misses — like part numbers, regulation codes, driver names, and technical specs.
Example: "DRS-2026" — only keywords work here
You search: "DRS-2026" (the drag reduction system regulation code)

📄 "...DRS-2026 eliminates the rear wing flap..." ✓ exact hit
📄 "...under DRS-2026, active aero replaces..." ✓ exact hit
📄 "...overtaking strategy and slipstream..." ✗ no match

Semantic search would miss "DRS-2026" entirely — it doesn't understand regulation codes. FTS5 finds it instantly.
Why BOTH are needed (Hybrid Search)
ChromaDB = "what does the question mean?" (F1 DRS → aerodynamic systems ✓)
FTS5 = "what exact words appear?" (DRS-2026 → exact hit ✓)

Cortex runs both searches in parallel, merges results, and sends the best chunks to GPT-4.1. This is called Hybrid Search — you get the best of both worlds.

Also includes enriched docs (Trinity-written deep explanations) scored higher than raw chunks.
